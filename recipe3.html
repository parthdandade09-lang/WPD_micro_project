Dal Makhani

Creamy, buttery black lentils slow-cooked to perfection

⏱️ Prep: 15 mins 🔥 Cook: 2 hours 🍽️ Serves: 4 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black urad dal (with skin)
  • 1/4 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  • 3 tbsp butter (divided)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Soak urad dal and rajma overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  • Pressure cook with 4 cups water, salt, and turmeric until soft (about 6-8 whistles).
  • Heat 2 tbsp butter and oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add onions and sauté until golden brown (8-10 minutes).
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté for 2 minutes.
  • Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates (5-7 minutes).
  • Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add cooked dal (mash slightly) and mix well. Add water for desired consistency.
  • Simmer on low flame for 45-60 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Add remaining butter and garam masala. Simmer for another 15 minutes.
  • Add fresh cream and mix well. Simmer for 5 more minutes.
  • Garnish with coriander and a drizzle of cream. Serve hot with naan or rice.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Slow cooking on low flame develops the authentic smoky flavor
  • Use unsalted butter for better control of seasoning
  • Add a teaspoon of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek) for restaurant-style flavor
  • Mash some dal against the pan to get creamy texture
  • Let the dal rest for 30 minutes before serving for flavors to meld
  • Use a heavy-bottomed pan to prevent burning

⚠️ Precautions

  • Stir frequently during slow cooking to prevent sticking
  • Don't skip soaking lentils; it ensures even cooking
  • Adjust water consistency carefully - dal thickens as it cools
  • Don't add cream at the beginning as it may curdle
