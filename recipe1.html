🥟 Samosa

Crispy, golden triangles filled with spiced potatoes and peas

⏱️ Prep: 30 mins 🔥 Cook: 20 mins 🍽️ Makes: 12 samosas ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • For Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • For Dough: 4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • For Dough: 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • For Dough: Salt to taste
  • For Dough: 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • For Filling: 4 large potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • For Filling: 1/2 cup green peas (boiled)
  • For Filling: 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • For Filling: 1 tsp fennel seeds (crushed)
  • For Filling: 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • For Filling: 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • For Filling: 1 tsp coriander powder
  • For Filling: 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • For Filling: 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • For Filling: 2 tbsp fresh coriander (chopped)
  • For Filling: Salt to taste
  • For Frying: Oil for deep frying

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, carom seeds, and ghee. Rub until mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  • Add water gradually and knead into a stiff dough. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
  • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add ginger and green chilies, sauté for 30 seconds.
  • Add mashed potatoes, peas, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, and salt. Mix well.
  • Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring continuously. Add fresh coriander and turn off heat. Let filling cool.
  • Divide dough into 6 equal balls. Roll each into a circle and cut into two halves.
  • Take one half, apply water on the straight edge, and form a cone shape.
  • Fill with 2 tbsp of potato mixture, seal the top edge with water.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan on medium flame. Fry samosas until golden and crisp.
  • Drain on paper towels and serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Use cold water while kneading dough for extra crispiness
  • Rest the dough for at least 30 minutes to make it pliable
  • Fry samosas on medium-low flame to ensure they cook evenly inside
  • Don't overfill the samosas, or they may burst while frying
  • Add a pinch of baking soda to dough for extra flakiness
  • Freeze uncooked samosas for later use - fry directly from frozen

⚠️ Precautions

  • Ensure oil is not too hot, otherwise samosas will brown outside but remain raw inside
  • Seal the edges properly to prevent filling from leaking during frying
  • Handle the dough gently - over-kneading can make samosas hard
  • Don't overcrowd the pan while frying; fry in batches
