🌀 Jalebi

Crispy, juicy, and sweet spirals - a classic Indian dessert

⏱️ Prep: 20 mins 🔥 Cook: 30 mins 🍽️ Makes: 20 jalebis ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • For Batter: 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • For Batter: 2 tbsp cornflour
  • For Batter: 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • For Batter: 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • For Batter: 1/2 cup water (adjust)
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1 cup sugar
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1/2 cup water
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1/4 tsp saffron strands
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1 tsp lemon juice
  • For Frying: Ghee or oil for deep frying

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • In a bowl, mix flour, cornflour, and baking soda. Add yogurt and mix well.
  • Add water gradually to make a smooth, thick batter (pouring consistency).
  • Cover and let the batter ferment for 8-10 hours or overnight.
  • For sugar syrup, combine sugar and water in a pan. Bring to a boil.
  • Add saffron, cardamom powder, and lemon juice. Simmer until one-string consistency.
  • Keep the syrup warm.
  • Heat ghee/oil in a deep pan on medium flame.
  • Fill the fermented batter in a piping bag or jalebi cloth with a small hole.
  • Pipe spirals directly into hot oil, making circular shapes.
  • Fry until crisp and golden on both sides (about 2-3 minutes per side).
  • Remove with a slotted spoon and immediately dip in warm sugar syrup for 30-40 seconds.
  • Remove from syrup and serve hot or at room temperature.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Fermentation is key - longer fermentation gives better texture
  • Batter should be thick but pourable; adjust consistency with water
  • Add a pinch of orange food color for restaurant-style jalebis
  • Keep sugar syrup warm, not hot, for perfect soaking
  • Use ghee for frying for authentic flavor and aroma
  • Don't overcrowd while frying; maintain consistent oil temperature

⚠️ Precautions

  • Don't overmix the batter after fermentation; it can deflate
  • Maintain medium flame while frying for even cooking
  • Don't soak jalebis too long or they become soggy
  • Ensure oil is hot enough before piping the batter
