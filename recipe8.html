🌯 Masala Dosa

Crispy rice crepe filled with spiced potato filling - South Indian classic

⏱️ Prep: 20 mins + fermentation 🔥 Cook: 30 mins 🍽️ Serves: 4 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • For Dosa Batter: 1 cup parboiled rice
  • For Dosa Batter: 1 cup regular rice
  • For Dosa Batter: 1/2 cup urad dal
  • For Dosa Batter: 1/4 cup chana dal (optional)
  • For Dosa Batter: 1 tbsp fenugreek seeds
  • For Dosa Batter: Salt to taste
  • For Potato Filling: 4 large potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • For Potato Filling: 1 large onion (sliced)
  • For Potato Filling: 2 green chilies (slit)
  • For Potato Filling: 1 tbsp ginger (grated)
  • For Potato Filling: 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • For Potato Filling: 1 tsp urad dal
  • For Potato Filling: 1 tsp chana dal
  • For Potato Filling: 10-12 curry leaves
  • For Potato Filling: 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • For Potato Filling: 2 tbsp oil
  • For Potato Filling: Fresh coriander (chopped)
  • For Serving: Coconut chutney & sambar

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Soak rice, fenugreek seeds for 6 hours. Soak urad dal and chana dal separately for 4 hours.
  • Grind urad dal with minimal water until fluffy and light.
  • Grind rice mixture to a slightly coarse paste.
  • Combine both batters, add salt, mix well. Ferment overnight (8-10 hours) until bubbly.
  • For potato filling: Heat oil, add mustard seeds, urad dal, chana dal. Let them splutter.
  • Add curry leaves, ginger, green chilies, onions. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  • Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well. Add coriander. Set aside.
  • Heat a non-stick tawa, sprinkle water to check temperature. Wipe clean.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter in the center, spread in circular motion to make thin dosa.
  • Drizzle oil/ghee around edges. Cook until golden and crisp.
  • Place potato filling in the center, fold the dosa. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Add a handful of cooked rice while grinding for extra crispiness
  • Fermentation time depends on climate; keep batter in warm place
  • Batter consistency should be like pancake batter - neither too thick nor too thin
  • Keep tawa on medium heat; too hot will burn dosa before spreading
  • Add fenugreek seeds for golden color and crispiness
  • Refrigerate leftover batter; bring to room temperature before using

⚠️ Precautions

  • Don't skip fermentation; it's essential for proper texture and digestibility
  • Ensure tawa is well-seasoned to prevent sticking
  • Don't spread batter on tawa that's too hot or too cold
  • Add salt only after fermentation for better results
