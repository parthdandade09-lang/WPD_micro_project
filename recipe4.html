🍗 Butter Chicken

Succulent chicken in a rich, creamy tomato gravy

⏱️ Prep: 20 mins 🔥 Cook: 40 mins 🍽️ Serves: 4 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • For Marination: 500g boneless chicken (cut into cubes)
  • For Marination: 1/2 cup yogurt
  • For Marination: 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • For Marination: 1 tsp red chili powder
  • For Marination: 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • For Marination: 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • For Marination: 1 tsp garam masala
  • For Marination: Salt to taste
  • For Gravy: 4 tbsp butter (divided)
  • For Gravy: 1 tbsp oil
  • For Gravy: 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • For Gravy: 4 tomatoes (pureed)
  • For Gravy: 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • For Gravy: 2 green chilies (slit)
  • For Gravy: 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • For Gravy: 1 tsp coriander powder
  • For Gravy: 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • For Gravy: 1 tsp kasuri methi
  • For Gravy: 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • For Gravy: 2 tbsp honey or sugar

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Mix all marination ingredients and coat chicken well. Refrigerate for 2-4 hours.
  • Heat 2 tbsp butter in a pan. Add marinated chicken and cook until browned (5-7 minutes). Set aside.
  • In the same pan, add remaining butter and oil. Add onions and sauté until golden (8-10 minutes).
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté for 2 minutes.
  • Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates (10-12 minutes).
  • Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 2 minutes.
  • Cool slightly and blend into a smooth puree. Strain back into pan.
  • Add cooked chicken and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  • Add honey/sugar, garam masala, and kasuri methi. Mix well.
  • Lower flame and add fresh cream. Stir gently.
  • Simmer for 5 minutes until gravy thickens to desired consistency.
  • Garnish with more cream and serve hot with naan or rice.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Use Kashmiri red chili powder for vibrant red color without too much heat
  • Straining the gravy gives a silky smooth texture
  • Add cream at the end and don't boil after adding to prevent curdling
  • Grill or tandoor the chicken for authentic smoky flavor
  • Use ripe, red tomatoes for natural sweetness and color
  • Adjust sweetness according to tomato tartness

⚠️ Precautions

  • Don't overcook chicken after adding to gravy; it becomes tough
  • Keep flame low when adding cream to avoid curdling
  • Don't skip marination time for tender chicken
  • Be careful while blending hot tomato mixture; let it cool slightly
