🥬 Shukto

Traditional Bengali mixed vegetable dish with a hint of bitterness

⏱️ Prep: 20 mins 🔥 Cook: 30 mins 🍽️ Serves: 4 ⭐ Difficulty: Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 small bitter gourd (karela, sliced)
  • 1 raw banana (sliced)
  • 1 potato (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup brinjal (eggplant, cubed)
  • 1/2 cup sweet potato (cubed)
  • 1/4 cup drumsticks (2-3 pieces)
  • 1/4 cup green papaya (cubed)
  • 1/4 cup bori (dried lentil dumplings)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp panch phoron (Bengali five-spice)
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp milk (to reduce bitterness)
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Wash and cut all vegetables into similar-sized pieces.
  • Rub bitter gourd with salt and turmeric, let sit for 10 minutes to reduce bitterness. Rinse well.
  • Heat mustard oil in a pan until it smokes. Add panch phoron and let it splutter.
  • Add bori and fry until golden. Remove and set aside.
  • In the same oil, add bitter gourd and fry until lightly browned. Remove and set aside.
  • Add remaining vegetables (except drumsticks) and sauté for 5-7 minutes.
  • Add ginger paste, turmeric powder, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  • Add drumsticks and 1 cup water. Cover and cook until vegetables are tender (10-12 minutes).
  • Add fried bitter gourd, bori, and milk. Simmer for 5 more minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with steamed rice.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Use mustard oil for authentic Bengali flavor
  • Rub bitter gourd with salt to reduce bitterness
  • Add milk at the end to balance the bitterness
  • Use a variety of vegetables for authentic taste
  • Don't overcook vegetables; they should retain some texture
  • Add prawns or fish head for non-vegetarian version

⚠️ Precautions

  • Heat mustard oil until smoking to remove raw smell
  • Don't skip the bitter gourd; it's essential for authentic flavor
  • Cut vegetables uniformly for even cooking
  • Add salt carefully as bori already contains salt
