🍜 Hakka Noodles

Stir-fried Indo-Chinese noodles with vegetables and savory sauces

⏱️ Prep: 15 mins 🔥 Cook: 20 mins 🍽️ Serves: 3 ⭐ Difficulty: Easy

🛒 Ingredients

  • 200g Hakka noodles (or any egg noodles)
  • 1/2 cup sliced carrots (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup sliced capsicum
  • 1/2 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 1/4 cup spring onions (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 cup shredded chicken or paneer

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Cook noodles according to package instructions. Drain, rinse with cold water, and toss with 1 tsp oil to prevent sticking.
  • Heat oil in a wok on high flame. Add ginger-garlic paste, sauté for 30 seconds.
  • Add all vegetables (reserve some spring onions for garnish) and stir-fry on high for 2-3 minutes.
  • If adding chicken/paneer, add now and stir-fry for 2 minutes.
  • Add soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Mix well.
  • Add cooked noodles, black pepper, and salt. Toss vigorously to combine.
  • Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until noodles are well-coated and slightly charred.
  • Garnish with spring onions and serve hot with Manchurian or chili sauce.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Rinse noodles with cold water after boiling to stop cooking and remove excess starch
  • Use high heat for authentic street-style Hakka noodles
  • Add a dash of sesame oil at the end for extra aroma
  • Don't overcook vegetables; they should remain crunchy
  • Use fresh noodles for best texture
  • Toss noodles with chopsticks or tongs for even mixing

⚠️ Precautions

  • Don't over-boil noodles; they become mushy when stir-fried
  • Keep flame high throughout cooking
  • Add sauces gradually and taste before adding salt
  • Ensure wok is very hot before adding ingredients
