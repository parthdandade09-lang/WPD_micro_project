🍬 Rasgulla

Soft, spongy cottage cheese balls soaked in sugar syrup

⏱️ Prep: 20 mins 🔥 Cook: 30 mins 🍽️ Makes: 12-15 rasgullas ⭐ Difficulty: Hard

🛒 Ingredients

  • For Chenna (Paneer): 1 liter full-fat milk
  • For Chenna: 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • For Chenna: 1 cup cold water
  • For Sugar Syrup: 2 cups sugar
  • For Sugar Syrup: 4 cups water
  • For Sugar Syrup: 2-3 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • For Sugar Syrup: 1 tsp rose water (optional)
  • For Dough: 1 tbsp semolina (sooji) or cornflour
  • For Dough: 1 tsp all-purpose flour (optional)

👩‍🍳 Procedure

  • Bring milk to a boil. Add lemon juice gradually while stirring until milk curdles completely.
  • Add cold water to stop curdling. Strain using muslin cloth and rinse with cold water to remove lemon taste.
  • Hang the cloth for 30-45 minutes to drain excess water. Chenna should be moist, not dry.
  • Transfer chenna to a plate. Knead with palm for 8-10 minutes until smooth and soft.
  • Add semolina and knead for 2 more minutes. Dough should be soft and non-sticky.
  • Divide into small lemon-sized balls. Roll between palms until smooth with no cracks.
  • In a wide pan, combine sugar and water. Bring to boil to make sugar syrup.
  • Add cardamom and rose water to syrup.
  • Gently drop chenna balls into boiling syrup. Cover and cook for 12-15 minutes.
  • Rasgullas will double in size. Check by pressing gently - they should be spongy.
  • Turn off heat and let them cool in syrup. Refrigerate and serve chilled.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Use full-fat milk for best results
  • Chenna should be moist - don't drain too much water
  • Knead until smooth; any graininess will result in hard rasgullas
  • Roll balls without any cracks to prevent breaking
  • Use a wide pan so rasgullas have space to expand
  • Add a little milk to syrup for extra softness

⚠️ Precautions

  • Don't over-drain chenna; it will become dry and rasgullas will be hard
  • Don't knead too little; it's essential for smooth texture
  • Don't crowd the pan; cook in batches if needed
  • Don't open lid frequently while cooking; they may deflate
